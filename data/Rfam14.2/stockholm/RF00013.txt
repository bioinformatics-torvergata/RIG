#=GF CC   E. coli 6S RNA was the first non coding RNA to be sequenced, but its
#=GF CC   function was unknown until recently. It consists of 184 nucleotides that
#=GF CC   fold into an extended hairpin structure with a large single-stranded
#=GF CC   internal bulge. The 6S RNA specifically associates with RNA polymerase
#=GF CC   holoenzyme containing the sigma70 specificity factor. This interaction
#=GF CC   represses expression from a sigma70-dependent promoter during stationary
#=GF CC   phase [1]. 6S RNA homologs have recently been identified in most bacterial
#=GF CC   genomes [2,3]. Many Gram-positive species have two copies of 6S RNA. In
#=GF CC   Bacillus subtilis, both copies appear to interact with RNA polymerase
#=GF CC   holoenzyme containing the housekeeping sigma factor and be expressed
#=GF CC   during different stages of growth. In many proteobacteria, 6S RNA may be
#=GF CC   processed from a transcript encoding homologs of the E. coli YgfA protein
#=GF CC   which is a putative methenyltetrahydrofolate synthetase.
