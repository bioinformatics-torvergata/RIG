#=GF CC   Protein translation of most eukaryotic mRNAs requires association of
#=GF CC   Met-tRNA, several initiation factors, and GTP with the 40S ribosomal
#=GF CC   subunit. The ribosome can only bind the capped mRNA after binding the
#=GF CC   initiator tRNA. Translation of hepatitis C virus (HCV) mRNA is initiated
#=GF CC   by a different mechanism from the usual 5' cap-binding model [2]. This
#=GF CC   alternate mechanism relies on the direct binding of the 40S ribosomal
#=GF CC   subunit by the internal ribosome entry site (IRES) in the 5' UTR of HCV
#=GF CC   RNA. HCV IRES adopts a complex structure, and may differ significantly
#=GF CC   from IRES elements identified in picornaviruses. A small number of
#=GF CC   eukaryotic mRNA have been shown to be translated by internal ribosome
#=GF CC   entry [3].
