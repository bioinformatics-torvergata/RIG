#=GF CC   This family represents a conserved RNA structure found upstream of the
#=GF CC   ykkC and yxkD genes in Bacillus subtilis and related genes in other
#=GF CC   bacteria. The function of this family is unclear although it has been
#=GF CC   suggested that it may function to switch on efflux pumps and
#=GF CC   detoxification systems in response to harmful environmental molecules [1].
#=GF CC   The Thermoanaerobacter tengcongensis sequence EMBL:AE013027 overlaps with
#=GF CC   Rfam:RF00167 suggesting that the two riboswitches may work in conjunction
#=GF CC   to regulate the the upstream gene which codes for Swiss:Q8RC62, a member
#=GF CC   of Pfam:PF00860 (Moxon SJ, pers. obs.). Nelson [2] showed that this
#=GF CC   riboswitch senses and responds to guanidine and it was renamed Guanidine-I
#=GF CC   riboswitch. Furthermore, they demonstrated that bacteria are capable of
#=GF CC   endogenously producing guanidine and the riboswitch controls genes whose
#=GF CC   products are involved in modification or pumping out guanidine as a toxic
#=GF CC   compound from bacteria. Crystal structures of the riboswitch bound to the
#=GF CC   ligand have also been determined.[4][5]
